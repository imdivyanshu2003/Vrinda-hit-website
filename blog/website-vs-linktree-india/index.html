If you're an Instagram creator in India — whether you run a Bhakti page, a fitness coaching account, or a small food business — you've probably been told: "put a Linktree in your bio." It's the default advice. But is it actually the right call in 2026?

Short answer: No. Linktree and Linkin.bio are fine starter tools, but they're costing Indian creators real leads, credibility, and revenue. Here's the detailed breakdown.

What is each tool, exactly?

Linktree

A free hosted page at linktr.ee/yourname that shows a list of clickable buttons. Great for beginners. Limited in design, branding, and analytics unless you pay $5–$24/month.

Linkin.bio

Later's alternative, integrated with Instagram post previews. Similar limitations to Linktree — you're renting space on someone else's platform.

Why link-in-bio tools hurt Indian creators

1. You're invisible on Google

Linktree pages don't rank. A real website with "dietitian in Delhi" or "Vrindavan seva booking" in the content can rank on Google, bringing you free organic leads every month. Linktree users depend 100% on Instagram.

2. You look like every other creator

Linktree's free template is instantly recognizable. Serious clients — especially for pooja bookings, fitness programs, and diet plans — want to see a branded experience, not a generic link list.

3. Weak conversion

A link-in-bio tool forces visitors to pick one of 8 buttons. A website guides them through a story: headline → benefits → proof → single CTA. That structure converts 3x–5x better.

4. No ownership

If Linktree changes pricing, disappears, or gets blocked, your entire funnel vanishes. Your website lives on a domain you own forever.

When Linktree actually makes sense

Linktree is fine if:

  • You're testing Instagram as a side hobby, not a business
  • You have under 500 followers and no monetization plan
  • You need a free 5-minute setup right now

Everyone else — including creators with 1,000+ followers, any form of monetization, or any services offered — is leaving leads and money on the table every month with a link-in-bio tool.
